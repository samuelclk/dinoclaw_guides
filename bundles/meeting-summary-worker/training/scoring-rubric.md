# Scoring Rubric: Samuel-Style Meeting Summary

Use this rubric to score Dino outputs against Samuel's preferred summary style.

## How to score

Score each category from **1 to 5**.

- **1** = poor / clearly wrong
- **2** = weak
- **3** = acceptable but needs work
- **4** = strong
- **5** = excellent / Samuel-style

Total possible score: **30**

## 1. Counterparty clarity

Question:
- Does the summary clearly identify who the meeting was with and why they matter?

High score looks like:
- clear first line
- role or useful context included when relevant
- no ambiguity about who the counterpart is

Low score signs:
- vague header
- missing role/context when needed
- hard to tell what type of partner this is

## 2. Signal selection

Question:
- Did the summary keep the important takeaways and cut the noise?

High score looks like:
- strong filtering
- only durable points survive
- key discussion points are truly consequential

Low score signs:
- too much fluff
- too much company background
- too much product explanation
- social/procedural clutter retained

## 3. Business framing

Question:
- Does the summary capture the real opportunity, blocker, dependency, or commercial angle?

High score looks like:
- emphasizes what changes action or prioritization
- preserves pivot / blocker diagnosis / dependency logic when present
- frames the meeting in terms Samuel can use later

Low score signs:
- descriptive but not useful
- misses the actual blocker
- misses the real reason the meeting mattered

## 4. Compression quality

Question:
- Is the summary concise without losing substance?

High score looks like:
- dense and readable
- no repeated points
- no over-explaining
- numbers only where useful

Low score signs:
- too long
- too sparse / missing substance
- rambling or repetitive

## 5. Next-step quality

Question:
- Are the next steps real, specific, and worth tracking?

High score looks like:
- owner + action where possible
- only genuine follow-ups retained
- clear operational utility

Low score signs:
- vague `explore further` language
- inflated or inferred actions
- too many housekeeping steps

## 6. Tone match

Question:
- Does it sound like Samuel-style internal notes rather than autogenerated minutes?

High score looks like:
- direct
- no fluff
- no meeting-minutes voice
- comfortable domain shorthand

Low score signs:
- sounds like Gemini or generic AI notes
- too polished/public-facing
- too much explanatory scaffolding

## Interpretation bands

- **27–30**: Ready to use
- **23–26**: Good, minor edits only
- **18–22**: Usable but needs notable tightening
- **12–17**: Weak; rewrite recommended
- **<12**: Off-style and low utility

## Fast review checklist

If short on time, ask:
- Did it preserve the real point of the meeting?
- Did it cut the noise?
- Are next steps worth tracking?
- Does it sound like Samuel?

If any answer is “no,” the draft needs revision.
