Summary
Samuel C and Fiona Ma discussed potential collaboration avenues between Lido and Fogen Finance, focusing on integrating stETH and Lido earn vaults into Fogen Finance's products and considering stETH as trading collateral for Fogen Finance's treasury assets. They reviewed fee sharing models, where Samuel C outlined a preferential fee structure on the Lido earn vaults based on Total Value Locked (TVL), and Fiona Ma raised the necessity of co-marketing efforts to gain internal support for integration. Samuel C and Fiona Ma also discussed the technical and compliance considerations of using stETH as collateral for minting USDF, the conversion of ETH reserves to stETH, and the distinction between Fogen Finance's yield products.


Details
* Introductions and Business Context Samuel C and Fiona Ma started the meeting with introductions, and Samuel C shared that they are with the institutional team at Lido and have been contributing to the protocol for two years. Samuel C mentioned that the institutional team focuses on speaking with various companies and asset managers regarding using ETH more efficiently while maintaining liquidity, or building on Lido. Fiona Ma confirmed that Samuel C had replaced a previous contact and that they were happy to move directly into the discussion.
* Potential Integration Avenues with Lido Samuel C outlined two main angles for collaboration with Fogen Finance: first, a potential integration of stETH (Lido's staked ETH) into Fogen Finance's products, or integration of Lido earn vaults onto Fogen Finance's earn platform. Second, Samuel C noted that Fogen Finance's strategy involves using its treasury assets (over 200 million ETH) as trading collateral and suggested considering using stETH instead due to its efficiency as trading collateral on various exchanges and OTC venues. Fiona Ma inquired if the rationale for the first option was primarily to increase the stETH use case.
* Integrating stETH and Lido Earn Vaults Samuel C confirmed that enabling stETH would increase Lido's Total Value Locked (TVL) by allowing more people to deploy ETH into stETH. Fiona Ma clarified that the TVL would be shared by both Lido and Fogen Finance, as Fogen Finance takes a fee from their users. Samuel C then introduced the Lido earn vaults, which offer more complex DeFi strategies with higher APRs (5% to 6% for leverage looping ones), managed by a DeFi curator. Fiona Ma asked if the second option meant displaying the Lido earn vault on Fogen Finance's website, which Samuel C confirmed, adding that Fogen Finance could package it as their own vault powered by Lido Earn.
* Fee Sharing and Preferential Arrangements Regarding how Fogen Finance could benefit, Samuel C explained that they could charge a fee using their own tag and that they could negotiate a preferential fee sharing mechanism on the Lido earn level, which unlocks at higher TVL tiers. Samuel C indicated that a tentative structure, subject to Lido earn committee approval, would be 10% of the 1% AUM fee on the vault at 20 million TVL, with tiers starting at 5% for 10 million AUM. Fiona Ma acknowledged the difficulty in guaranteeing TVL but noted that the team would need to consider the fee structure further.
* stETH as Minting Collateral and Co-Marketing Samuel C also brought up a third angle, which was allowing stETH to be used as collateral to mint USDF, as Fiona Ma had originally mentioned. Fiona Ma noted that adding stETH as collateral is relatively easy to consider. A significant factor for Fiona Ma's team to push for integration internally would be a joint announcement or co-marketing push with Lido, which Samuel C stated would need to be checked with the marketing team due to past difficulties, though they agreed on the value of early announcements.
* Technical and Compliance Considerations Fiona Ma confirmed that if stETH is enabled as collateral, the stETH would show up as one of the reserves. Samuel C questioned if Fogen Finance would be able to convert ETH in their reserves to stETH, which Fiona Ma stated needed internal discussion, especially considering potential compliance or community communication issues if a user's collateral was secretly swapped from ETH to stETH. Samuel C suggested checking if it would be cleaner to create a new collateral type, as other protocols do.
* Yield Product Distinction and Other Strategies Fiona Ma clarified the difference between their classic yield and boosted yield products: classic yield allows immediate staking/unstaking, while boosted yield offers higher yield but with a lock duration. Samuel C also mentioned a "DeFi interface," a middle layer that simplifies the integration of various DeFi yield products onto any platform, which could be used if Fogen Finance wants to bring non-ETH-based yield strategies.
* Priorities for Lido and Next Steps Samuel C outlined Lido's priority in terms of value: first, converting some of Fogen Finance's ETH reserves to stETH for their purposes; second, enabling stETH as minting collateral; and third, integrating the staking vaults. Fiona Ma viewed enabling stETH as minting collateral (priority two) as the easiest, but noted that priorities one and three require internal discussion, particularly concerning current trading strategy allocations and compliance. The meeting concluded with Samuel C agreeing to share documentation and follow up on the co-marketing possibility.
