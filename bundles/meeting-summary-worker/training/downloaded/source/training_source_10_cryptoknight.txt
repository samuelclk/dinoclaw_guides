* Introduction to Lido and Liquid Staking Samuel C introduced Lido as the largest liquid staking protocol in the industry with approximately 28 billion USD in Total Value Locked (TVL). Samuel C explained that liquid staking with Lido allows users to stake their ETH while receiving a liquid receipt token called ST, which remains usable across the DeFi ecosystem, serving as high-quality collateral for activities such as borrowing or leverage staking. They also noted that ST can be used as productive trading collateral on various exchanges, continuing to generate staking rewards while trades are being executed.
* ST Liquidity and Withdrawal Mechanisms Samuel C highlighted the high liquidity of ST, stating there is typically 75 million to 100 million in instant liquidity available within a 2% spread. They detailed two withdrawal methods: instantly via decentralized exchanges (Dexes) within 1 to 5 minutes, or a native withdrawal from the protocol which can be as short as one day, significantly shorter than the Ethereum native withdrawal queue which takes at least 9 to 10 days. The faster native withdrawal is possible because new people entering the Lido protocol can offset those who are exiting, bypassing the full Ethereum exit process.
* Lido Protocol Risks and Mitigation Zachary Young raised questions about the risks associated with Lido, including smart contract risk and leverage. Samuel C acknowledged that all DeFi protocols carry smart contract risk, but noted that Lido has invested over 4 million USD in security efforts, including 70+ audit reports, and operates as an open-source protocol benefiting from continuous peer reviews. Furthermore, Samuel C explained that Lido is a fully on-chain protocol governed by LDO token holders, utilizing a dual governance framework where ST holders can veto malicious token holder votes, providing a safeguard for users.
* Lido DeFi Interface for Integrators Samuel C presented the Lido DeFi interface as a way for Atlantis to offer various DeFi strategies to their users with minimal integration effort, ideally a single line of code, such as an iframe HTML. This solution allows Atlantis to customize branding, set fees, and own the user relationship, while the Lido DeFi interface team handles the backend security, maintenance, and future proofing. They also mentioned that Chris had already spoken with Ivan from the DeFi interface team about this.
* Benefits and Security Features of the DeFi Interface Samuel C outlined additional benefits of using the DeFi interface, including simplified accounting for fee splits, attribution for personalized incentives, and future-proofing for new DeFi strategy integration. A key security feature is the ability for the integrator, as the vault administrator, to trigger an emergency withdrawal function for users' funds within the strategy vault in the event of an exploit, although the end-users retain ownership of their assets.
* Commissions and Available Strategies Samuel C explained that commissions can be set on various levels, including deposit/withdrawal, percentage of rewards, or a percentage of AUM/TVL. Available blue-chip strategies include vanilla ST staking and Lido earn vaults, as well as USDC and potentially XSGD vaults, which Samuel C noted would be a strong addition for Atlantis's likely Singaporean user base. Samuel C confirmed that the staking is non-custodial, meaning users retain control of their assets.
* Revenue Potential and Early Adopter Commissions Samuel C provided an estimated revenue model based on a scenario of 100 million TVL flowing into the Atlantis earn product, projecting a total annual revenue of approximately 300K from combining fees on the DeFi interface level and fee-share revenue from the underlying strategy. Zachary Young confirmed understanding of the volume-based earning model. Samuel C also noted that early adopters, which Atlantis is being considered for, will receive 12 months of commission-free usage, after which commissions will be split between the integrator and Lido.
* Atlantis Product Development Timeline and Strategy Zachary Young outlined the Atlantis development plan, noting they are currently in Phase 2, focused on improving Phase 1 basic features, which should conclude within a couple of weeks, potentially by the end of January or beginning of February. Zachary Young indicated that Phase 3 would focus on new features, with the staking product expected to be included there, and mentioned the possibility of delaying Phase 2 due to Chinese New Year and other trips.
* The Search for a "Sticky" Feature for Atlantis Zachary Young shared that a key challenge for Atlantis, which is essentially a super app aggregator built upon other wallets, is finding a "sticky" feature that will make users keep coming back, akin to the payment feature of AliPay or the chatting feature of WeChat. They identified potential sticky features as a portfolio aggregator for tracking total holdings and, more specifically, a daily notification system showing users how much they are earning from staking across different protocols. Samuel C agreed that the accounting module of the Lido DeFi interface already handles the necessary data to support the daily earnings notification feature, and offered to check with the team on the ease of implementation.
* Long-Term Vision for Atlantis Zachary Young stated that while the Cryptonite community is the launchpad for Atlantis, the end goal is not to be limited by that growth but to attract users from other countries and expand the user base. Zachary Young and Samuel C discussed the challenges of incorporating payment features due to licensing and KYC requirements, which can slow down adoption. Zachary Young emphasized that the simplest features often win, and they aim to develop a simple feature that everyone can adopt quickly.
