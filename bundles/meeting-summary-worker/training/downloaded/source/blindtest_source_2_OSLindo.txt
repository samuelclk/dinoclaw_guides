Summary
Samuel C and Muhammad Zaky discussed a potential collaboration between Lido and OSL Indonesia, with Samuel introducing Lido's institutional focus on capital-efficient ETH utilization, stETH products, and the "Lido Earn" strategy. Muhammad Zaky proposed using a "Launchpool" or "hold and earn" product to feature stETH due to Indonesian regulatory obstacles regarding "actual staking" permits and suggested that OSL Indonesia could implement a brokerage model to leverage OSL Global's liquidity for stETH. They concluded that while listing stETH or offering a hold-and-earn campaign is constrained by current listing requirements and regulatory concerns, both parties showed interest in event collaborations, such as a high-end dinner for high-net-worth individuals and OSL's yearly Coinfest event in Bali in August.


Details
* Introductions and Background Samuel C and Muhammad Zaky, COO for OSL Indonesia, connected through Vincent and established that Zaky is based in BSD, Tangerang, Indonesia, while Samuel is Malaysian. Zaky confirmed that listing is under their oversight at OSL Indonesia. Samuel confirmed their prior understanding that OSL had acquired or rebranded from coins. Zaky clarified that the entire team has been OSL since the beginning, but they had initially operated under a separate brand, with the current directive being a unified OSL brand across different regions (Japan, Indonesia, Australia).
* Lido's Institutional Focus and Product Offerings Samuel explained that they work with Lido's institutional team, dealing with exchanges, custodians, and asset managers on two main topics: capital-efficient utilization of ETH, and building products around the Staked ETH (stETH) token. Samuel also introduced the "Lido Earn" strategy, which brings curated DeFi strategies, primarily involving leverage staking, to users. This leverage staking involves users using stETH as collateral on protocols like Aave, Fluid, or Spark to borrow more ETH to stake again, achieving a higher APY (5.5%) compared to basic stETH staking (2.6%).
* stETH Listing and Redemption Methods Samuel noted that stETH listing is one option for exchanges, and another is enabling minting and redemption of stETH because stETH holders automatically accrue staking rewards. Redemption through the protocol takes six days but is still faster than native withdrawals on Ethereum, as deposits can offset exits. Instant redemption can be achieved by directing users to a DEX, which takes 1 to 5 minutes. Samuel explained that monetization through this path is possible if the exchange has a Web3 wallet that integrates with DEXs like Uniswap, allowing the exchange to earn affiliate fees for swaps.
* Challenges with stETH Listing and Launchpool Product Samuel explained that stETH listing is costly for Lido due to the requirement to pay market makers to maintain liquidity for stETH pairs, and listing is rarely pursued unless the exchange has very good trading volumes. Muhammad Zaky stated their interest in a staking product, but highlighted a regulatory obstacle in Indonesia: communicating a product as "actual staking" requires a specific permit that OSL Indonesia does not yet have. Instead, Zaky proposed utilizing a "Launchpool" product, similar to Binance's model years ago, as no Indonesian exchange currently offers one. They are considering using USDC as a reward, derived from managing funds through connections to OSL Global's futures product, which avoids locking user assets, addressing a key regulatory concern against being classified as staking.
* Addressing Liquidity and Fees for stETH Integration Samuel proposed that enabling minting and redemption of stETH could be the easiest path forward, allowing instant receipt of stETH in the user's wallet and free transfer/swap if the funds are not locked. Muhammad Zaky confirmed that not locking the funds is key to compliance. Samuel provided data on stETH's current near-instant liquidity, noting that $75-80 million USD is available with a low spread. Muhammad Zaky confirmed that charging a withdrawal or redeem fee for instant withdrawal might be possible, which Samuel believes would solve the economics problem as the product scales by covering potential spread.
* Competitive Landscape and Staking License Samuel noted that they have spoken with other Indonesian exchanges already doing staking, specifically naming Indodax and MUV, with MUV already utilizing Lido for a combined staking vault. Muhammad Zaky explained that acquiring the staking license requires entering the sandbox and being verified, but it has not been OSL Indonesia's priority yet. Samuel observed that a launchpool product might be considered more regulated than staking, leading Zaky to suggest potentially rebranding the product as a "hold and earn campaign" or "join and earn" to manage external communication carefully.
* OSL Indonesia's Market and Collaboration Opportunities Muhammad Zaky reported OSL Indonesia's daily trading volume is around $1.5 to $2 million on a good day, with a target to stabilize above $2 million by the end of Q1, supported by OSL Global's focus on Indonesia. Samuel proposed a collaboration model for stETH listing where OSL Indonesia's internal trading desk runs a brokerage model, providing liquidity through OSL Global or other global exchanges where Lido has deep liquidity (e.g., Bybit or OKX), thus solving the liquidity problem for Lido. This would allow stETH users on OSL Indonesia to automatically accumulate staking rewards, with OSL potentially taking a fee from the APR before distribution.
* Roadshow and Event Collaboration Samuel expressed interest in a roadshow collaboration in Indonesia, leveraging their connections from previous work in Southeast Asian VC, to engage with high-net-worth individuals and family offices. Muhammad Zaky invited Lido to explore collaboration at OSL's yearly Coinfest event in Bali in August, usually a casual networking party. Samuel suggested a more high-end event like a dinner for the HNW segment to showcase an active integration. Zaky confirmed that OSL's internal market maker, owned by the same group, handles liquidity for global exchange listings and could support IDR to ETH to stETH pairs if the brokerage model between OSL Indonesia and OSL Global is feasible.
* Product Roadmap and Token Listing Requirements Samuel and Zaky discussed that while OSL Global is a potential liquidity bridge, the listing of stETH is constrained because the Hong Kong SSC does not allow the token to be listed yet. Zaky confirmed that the market maker they use is present on Bitget, where stETH is listed, and will follow up with the global listing team. Regarding the "hold and earn" product, Zaky noted that using a variety of tokens as rewards is safer than just stablecoins, which regulators might see as competition to banks. They currently only list USDC and USDT. Zaky clarified that any token must be listed first before it can be added to the "hold and earn" program, as they want users to trade the token afterward, which applies to stETH listing. Muhammad Zaky concluded that OSL is still very early in its product development phase, only one month after rebranding, and their primary goal as an exchange is to maximize trading volumes.
