Details
Introductions and Roles Samuel C and Hery Hermawan began the meeting with introductions, establishing that Samuel C is in Singapore and Hery Hermawan is in Jakarta, Indonesia. Hery Hermawan explained their role as Head of Product Development (PD) for a mobile exchange, where their responsibilities cover listing, market making, and partnerships, including those with validators and DeFi protocols.
Current Use of Lido Finance Hery Hermawan confirmed that they already utilize Lido Finance to deploy their Ethereum users' assets for their "simple earn" product. They consolidate user Ethereum and deploy it to the backend, splitting the deployment 50/50 between Lido and Ether, noting Ether offers a slightly higher Annual Percentage Rate (APR) but has an unlock period. They only offer basic LST (Liquid Staking Token) staking at 2.5% to their users.
Staking Volume and Listing Status Hery Hermawan estimated that the total amount currently staked in their "simple earn" (which is the same as "flexi earn") product is about 5,000 ETH, though the total circulation of ETH held by users is much larger. Samuel C inquired about listing stETH (Lido's LST) on their mobile exchange, to which Hery Hermawan replied it is not yet listed but expressed interest in listing stETH.
Lido Earn and DeFi Strategies Samuel C introduced the newly launched Lido Earn, a product they suggested the exchange should consider, noting it has accumulated close to 300 million TVL silently. Lido Earn utilizes DeFi vaults managed by curators, who are D5 fund managers, to deploy ETH and maximize APY within security constraints. The main strategy for one vault is leveraged staking, where stETH is used as collateral on protocols like Aave to borrow native ETH, which is then re-staked, potentially ramping up the APY.
Curators and Credibility Hery Hermawan sought clarification on the role of curators, and Samuel C explained they are like fund managers who decide how to deploy the assets. Curators like Melo (for one vault) need licensing and credibility to work with Lido. The curators primarily run leveraged ETH staking and farming incentives to increase APY without excessive risk exposure.
Lido DeFi Interface and Commercial Terms Samuel C proposed using the Lido DeFi interface to bring Lido Earn to the exchange's users, which is currently going through final audits and will be ready by the end of the month. This interface, described as "Stripe for DeFi," aggregates different DeFi strategies, allowing the integrator (the mobile exchange) to retain the user relationship and set commissions/fees. For the first 12 months, the integrator keeps 100% of the set commissions, after which a split will be negotiated based on factors like anticipated TVL and go-to-market plan.
Integration and Technical Details The integration of the D5 interface can be as simple as a single line of code, with the Lido team handling security, backend maintenance, and updating D5 strategies. Samuel C clarified that the interface supports offering vanilla stETH staking, Lido Earn vaults, and third-party vaults like those from Mofo for USDC. For the Lido Earn vault, fee sharing with the integrator starts at a minimum of 5 million USD in TVL, with tiers up to 10% share of the 1% TBL fee at 20 million USD TVL.
Non-Custodial Nature and Regulatory Compliance Hery Hermawan indicated that they do not want to display an external partnership like Lido due to regulator concerns and prefer a model where their engineers deploy the user Ethereum to Lido Earn. Samuel C confirmed that the Lido Earn and D5 interface are non-custodial, meaning the assets remain under the exchange's custody in the case of their users' non-custodial interaction. Samuel C argued that using the D5 interface, even privately, is more elegant than a direct allocation because it enables the exchange to receive additional fee shares negotiated by Lido with underlying D5 providers, avoiding "leaving money on the table".
Trading Volume and stETH Listing Hery Hermawan estimated the exchange's daily trading volume to be between 50 to 60 million USD, with ETH accounting for 20-25% of that, making it a sizable market. Regarding listing stETH, Samuel C noted that listing is an expensive process and they would need to evaluate market making costs, but Hery Hermawan offered free listing if a co-marketing budget could be split. A challenge with listing stETH is that it is a yield token whose balance increases daily, which can complicate custodial management and regulatory aspects.
Next Steps and Operational Details Hery Hermawan requested that Samuel C share the deck and commercial fee details, and Samuel C agreed to connect Hery Hermawan with the D5 interface team for commercial negotiations and whitelisting. Hery Hermawan inquired about liquidity and withdrawal periods for Lido Earn. Samuel C confirmed there is a withdrawal period of up to 72 hours and a deposit period of up to 24 hours, and the APR fluctuates based on the underlying strategies.
