Summary
Mingshi Song of Startail Group, which is building an Ethereum L2 called Sonium with Sony and a layer-one chain "Stream" with SBI, met with Samuel C from the institutional team at Lido and NK Yoon to explore potential synergies and collaborations in the APAC region. Samuel C is focused on institutions using STETH for capital efficiency and suggested that Startail's upcoming bank-issued JPY stablecoin could create opportunities for carry trade and liquidity unlock when using STETH as collateral, a use case that Mingshi Song confirmed interest in, specifically for Astar's vaults on top of Morpho JPY Save Vaults. The participants discussed advanced product integration, including structured products on top of STETH, the potential integration of the Lido Earn product by Astar or SBI, and agreed to prioritize collaboration around the Morpho vault JPY stablecoin borrowing.

Details
Introductions and Technical Difficulties Mingshi Song, based in Chandu, and NK Yoon, based in Korea, met with Samuel C, who is in Singapore. The meeting initially faced technical difficulties, with Mingshi Song noting a lag, and NK Yoon unable to hear Samuel C at one point. They successfully transitioned to Telegram to resolve the audio issues.
Lido and Startail Group Background Samuel C shared that they are with the institutional team at Lido, contributing for the past two years, covering the APEC region after a prior team member left. Mingshi Song explained that Startail Group was founded by the founder of Astar network, and they have a history with Lido, primarily through past contacts like Jacov. Samuel C focuses on conversations with institutions regarding using STETH for capital efficiency or building on top of Lido/STETH to leverage network effects.
Startail Group's Major Focus Areas Mingshi Song, leading DeFi for Startail, detailed the company's major initiatives. These include Sonium, a joint venture with Sony to build an Ethereum L2 for entertainment use cases, and joint ventures with SBI, a major Japanese financial group, to build the layer-one chain "Stream" for tokenized assets and Omni trading. A key project with SBI is the JPY stablecoin, which will be the first bank-issued JPY stablecoin under a trust bank license, allowing for institutional and broader DeFi/TradFi use cases, unlike the existing JPYC which is limited to a ¥1 million transfer cap under the electronic payment act.
Potential Synergies in the APAC Region Samuel C noted their on-the-ground work in Hong Kong, China, Japan, and Korea, aiming to explore collaboration opportunities with Startail. Their institutional focus in APAC revolves around staking, specifically speaking with Japanese DAT companies that want to use Lido STETH for liquidity or to amplify staking rewards through strategies like leverage staking and collateralization against stablecoins. Samuel C suggested that Startail's collaboration with SBI to issue JPY stablecoins could facilitate interesting use cases for carry trade exposure and liquidity unlock when using STETH as collateral.
Collaboration Opportunities with Startail and Astar Mingshi Song confirmed the interest in the JPY stablecoin collateralization use case, mentioning that Astar, a Startail associate and successful Japanese native blockchain project, will be curating vaults on top of Morpho JPY Save Vaults. Mingshi Song indicated that Astar, being more flexible in development resources and focusing on web3-native products, could be a strong candidate for collaboration on strategies and development. Samuel C elaborated that current institutional borrowing in Japan is still focused on USD-based loans, with interest rates between 9% and 11% via custodians, offering a 24-hour window to fulfill margin requirements upon liquidation threshold breach.
Exploring Advanced Product Integration and Off-chain Structures Mingshi Song proposed pitching together to DAT companies about borrowing JPY stablecoins, potentially leveraging Startail's off-ramp channels for converting back to fiat JPY. Samuel C suggested exploring structured products on top of STETH for traditional asset managers, mentioning a discussion with SBI's Singapore arm about securitizing the Ethena basis trade model where the spot leg is held in STETH to enhance yield. For off-chain implementation of structured products, Samuel C noted that this would require an actively managed certificate (AMC) structure, which involves compliance and regulatory frameworks rather than just tech.
Lido Earn and Market Strategy Samuel C introduced Lido Earn, a new product offering 5-6% yield via easy-to-use vaults with different ETH-based DeFi strategies, similar to a Morpho vault, which could be integrated into a web3 wallet or self-custodial interface by Astar or SBI. Samuel C indicated that an integration of Lido Earn could provide a revenue driver via fee sharing if TVL thresholds are met. Mingshi Song expressed high interest in stablecoin-based strategies, as most Japanese enterprises currently do not hold ETH or other crypto assets on their balance sheets, though Samuel C noted recent news about large Japanese asset managers pushing to be allowed to hold digital assets.
Business Development and Next Steps Mingshi Song prioritized collaboration around the Morpho vault JPY stablecoin borrowing as the easiest starting point over longer institutional co-BD efforts. NK Yoon provided context on the current status for Japanese asset managers to hold crypto, stating a possible path exists for non-traditional companies like Mai Capital via a local GKTK fund structure. Samuel C agreed to open discussions with Japanese DAT companies about the JPY stablecoin option and suggested connecting with traditional asset managers who hold ETH. NK Yoon mentioned that, although they are aware of David related to Base, they are not currently prioritizing integration with the Coinbase stablecoin ecosystem, though Mingshi Song expressed interest in further discussion.
Japan Fintech Week and Stablecoin Timeline Samuel C inquired about participation in a Japan Fintech Week round table with the SFA, but NK Yoon clarified that Startail is not directly involved, and Samuel C should reach out to the JFSA contacts directly. Regarding the JPY stablecoin (code name JPY SC), Mingshi Song clarified that it has not been issued yet, as they are waiting for approval from the FSA, with an expected launch date around June 2026. Samuel C confirmed they would follow up with information and slides on integration directions and requested more details on JPY SC, despite the future launch date.
