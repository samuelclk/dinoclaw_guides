Summary
Samuel C., the Institutional Relations Lead at Lido, and Jeremy Lim, the strategy and partnerships lead at SEU, discussed the low client uptake of staking products on SEU and explored opportunities to increase the market share for Lido's stETH. Jeremy Lim explained that SEU offers the stETH product, but they noted that staking on their platform generally has low uptake, which they speculate may be due to SEU being late to the market or the returns not being competitive enough (00:04:43). Exploration of Opportunities and Client Services: Samuel C. offered to help SEU increase their market share for staking services by running webinars or initiating business development (BD) activities to educate clients on the benefits of liquid staking (00:06:34). When asked about SEU’s most in-demand feature besides custody, Jeremy Lim specified that it is their external exchange (OES) solutions with partners like Binance and Coolcoin. They further detailed SEU's two models: Mirror X, the direct OES solution, and Mirror Reserve, which targets tokens not supported on the exchange but still used as collateral for futures and margin trading (00:07:41).
Use of stETH as Trading Collateral: Samuel C. inquired whether stETH could be used as collateral under SEU's model, and Jeremy Lim confirmed that it is possible but that the Loan-to-Value (LTV) and margin haircut would need to be confirmed with the exchange partner, Binance (00:08:30). Jeremy Lim explained that for SEU to accept stETH as collateral, they would need a committed or rough target for Assets Under Management (AUM) to present to the Binance team for approval of collateral recognition and margins (00:09:20). Mirror X (spot, margin, and futures trading) is currently active with Coolcoin and Binance, while Mirror Reserve (collateral management for unlisted tokens) is currently live only on Binance (00:10:08).
Collateral Recognition and Use Cases: Jeremy Lim noted that while they cannot push exchanges to list assets, they can push for the recognition of assets staked in SEU as collateral, provided there is sufficient demand from clients. Samuel C. shared that they have large fund clients exploring using stETH as collateral on exchanges for delta-neutral positions, such as funding arbitrage, or to collateralize and borrow stablecoins for trading (00:10:51). Jeremy Lim expressed caution about using Mirror Reserve for delta-neutral strategies, as it operates on a loan model that requires paying for portfolio margin, which could diminish the thin spreads of such trades (00:11:37).
Current Staking Product Challenges: Jeremy Lim stated that they are generally open to opportunities but noted that they have several existing staking projects with no AUM, which led them to establish a new policy to only integrate new products if there is clear client demand (00:12:30). For already-integrated products like Lido, Jeremy Lim emphasized the need to find compelling use cases to attract clients, suggesting that if stETH can offer clients collateral on Binance, that could create a strong argument for uptake (00:13:22). Jeremy Lim clarified that SEU only has Lido's stETH and the Binance-based WBETH as Ethereum staking options (00:14:48).
Mirror X vs. Mirror Reserve and stETH Position: Jeremy Lim confirmed that Lido's stETH is not currently listed on the Binance exchange but is active on the Binance Wallet, meaning that it falls under the Mirror Reserve model for collateral use (00:18:02) (00:19:40). They explained that Mirror X (omnibus account) is generally more efficient, but Mirror Reserve (qualified wallet) is necessary for clients who require segregated, on-chain transparency for auditing purposes (00:15:23). Jeremy Lim confirmed that they will not compete with Lido's client referrals and will use the Mirror Reserve path for clients bringing in stETH for collateral use on Binance (00:17:07).
Next Steps for Collaboration: Samuel C. proposed two immediate next steps: partnering with Jeremy Lim on pitches for clients interested in using stETH as collateral for trading on Binance, and educating SEU's client base on stETH use cases through a sharing session or webinar (00:17:07). Jeremy Lim emphasized the need for a sufficiently large sum, likely exceeding $5–$10 million, or a high trading volume from the client, for Binance to recognize stETH as collateral (00:19:40). Jeremy Lim offered to arrange a short call with their sales team so they can be introduced to Samuel C. and understand who to contact with questions about Lido (00:20:19).
