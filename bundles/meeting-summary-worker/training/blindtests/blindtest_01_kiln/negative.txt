Personal Trip Recap and Return to Work: Samuel C and Cherry Yim opened the meeting with a discussion of Samuel C's recent two-week holiday following Japan Fintech week, which included a four-day snowboarding trip and sightseeing in Kamakura and Tokyo (00:00:00). CoinShares Partnership and Upcoming New York Focus: Cherry Yim shared the positive news that they had announced a partnership with CoinShares, who will be the first "blue-chip DeFi" entity to use their stack to build launching vaults. Cherry Yim noted that the team is currently focused on an upcoming trip to New York next week to meet with LPs, funds, exchanges, and others interested in the space (00:01:41).
Real Net Protocol and Infrastructure Role: Samuel C inquired about the nature of Real Net, and Cherry Yim clarified that it is a protocol that functions as programmable infrastructure, allowing asset managers or creators to structure on-chain financing solutions. Real Net positions itself as an infrastructure provider and does not manage assets, underwrite borrowing, or engage in lending, choosing instead to outsource the risk and creation to fund managers and experts like CoinShares or Centrifuge (00:02:40).
Real Net's Differentiation and RWA Inclusion: Cherry Yim explained that Real Net's stack is designed to be inclusive of both synchronous DeFi markets and Real World Assets (RWAs), which operate on an asynchronous flow, allowing regulated entities to unify both for building products (00:04:39). While the initial phase will focus on easier, fully on-chain vaults, the plan is to incorporate RWAs into strategies in the second phase, with the infrastructure being built to accommodate future inclusion if desired by asset managers (00:05:59).
Discussion on Decentralized Autonomous Treasury (DAT) Needs: Samuel C mentioned that DAT companies are pivoting away from pure ETH strategies and are looking for DeFi vaults that can manage the logic of both on-chain and RWA components, seeking ways to differentiate themselves from competitors like Bitmain (00:05:59). The ability to diversify their portfolio mix away from just ETH, potentially including RWAs or moving the JPY/USD carry on-chain, would allow them to generate better risk-adjusted returns (00:07:19).
Strategy for Combining ETH and RWA in a Single Vault: Samuel C asked if Real Net's DeFi vault could manage both ETH (via stETH) and RWA strategies concurrently within the same vault. Cherry Yim confirmed that the goal is to have all strategies within a single vault, but the actual strategy implementation and finding the alpha would be the responsibility of the portfolio manager or a curator like Centrifuge (00:09:29).
Potential Collaboration with DAT Companies: Cherry Yim proposed that if the DAT companies are interested in understanding how their RWA strategy could work, they could arrange a discovery call that includes the product team to determine how Real Net's infrastructure could support their needs (00:10:34). Samuel C agreed to share more information on RWA settlement once available and to reach out to DAT companies in the meantime (00:11:50) (00:19:57).
Regulatory Hurdles for Liquid Staking in Japan: Samuel C brought up the topic of BitBank and GMO Coin moving towards liquid staking, to which Cherry Yim responded that they have raised this with various Japanese exchanges. The main concern from the regulators, particularly cited by SBI, appears to be that exchanges are currently not allowed to hold liquid staking tokens like stETH on their balance sheet without submitting for and receiving regulatory approval (00:11:50).
The Value Proposition of stETH Vaults for Exchanges: Samuel C provided a specific use case for stETH vaults, explaining that they would enable exchanges to offer quicker withdrawals for users without needing to hold idle ETH on their balance sheets to underwrite risk (00:13:56). Cherry Yim acknowledged that using stETH on the exchange's own balance sheet might face fewer regulatory hurdles than offering it directly to users, but noted that Japanese exchanges like Crypto Garage are currently more concerned with entry queue liquidity than withdrawal times (00:15:13).
Strategy for Engaging Japanese Exchanges: The participants agreed that continuous engagement and explanation of the liquid staking use case are necessary, as the Japanese exchanges may not yet fully understand the potential benefits. They plan to utilize the return of Haruki, who can communicate the value proposition in Cantonese or Japanese, to better explain the advantages of stETH to local partners, including GMO Coin, where they are already providing native staking (00:16:13) (00:19:02).
Next Steps with Coin Check and Follow-up Meetings: Samuel C and Cherry Yim discussed pursuing a deal with Coin Check, who uses Block Daemon (which does not use stETH vaults) and is open to switching (00:17:19). They planned to schedule a call with Haruki upon their return next week to brief them and prepare for continued discussions with Japanese partners (00:18:05).
